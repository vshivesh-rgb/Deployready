Why DeployReady

What makes DeployReady different.

Four things that distinguish how we work from how most AI engagements are run.

—
Deep implementation experience

The DeployReady team comprises senior consultants with hands-on AI implementation experience at organisations including McKinsey, Accenture, and HCL. Every engagement is led by practitioners who have designed, deployed, and stabilised AI systems inside large enterprises — not advisors who hand off the hard work to junior teams.

—
India-specific regulatory depth

Our work is built around the regulatory frameworks Indian enterprises operate under — DPDP Act 2023, RBI Model Risk Management guidelines, IRDAI algorithmic decision requirements, MeitY AI governance guidelines, and NABH standards. This depth is embedded from the start of every engagement, not added as a compliance review at the end.

—
End-to-end delivery architecture

DeployReady engagements are structured across the full AI adoption lifecycle — from maturity diagnosis and use case strategy through deployment, post-launch monitoring, and internal capability handover. Each stage has a defined output, and the engagement closes only when those outputs are in place.

—
Deployment and orchestration focus

Most AI engagements stop at strategy or prototype. DeployReady is structured around the deployment outcome — the production system, the governance documentation, the monitoring pipeline, the internal team that can sustain it. That focus on production readiness shapes every decision we make across an engagement.

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 has direct implications for any AI system that processes personal data — which includes most customer-facing and HR applications. In practice this means consent frameworks need to be built into data pipelines, algorithmic decisions affecting individuals require documented justification, and data localisation requirements affect where models can be trained and where outputs can be stored. DeployReady addresses these requirements as part of the governance stage — as engineering and documentation decisions embedded in the deployment workflow, not as a legal opinion.
DeployReady functions as an orchestration and governance layer alongside internal teams. The diagnostic establishes what internal teams are already doing well, where gaps exist, and how the engagement should be structured to complement existing work. The capability handover at the end of the engagement is designed specifically to strengthen internal ownership so the organisation is less dependent on external support over time.
The full implementation engagement includes 90 days of post-launch monitoring and hyper-care support as part of the standard scope. The engagement closes with a 12-month AI roadmap and a full internal capability handover — structured so the organisation can sustain and scale what was built. Advisory retainer arrangements are available for organisations that want continued access to the team, but they are not a requirement of the engagement model.
